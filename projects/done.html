DONE.
A woman urges a bus driver to depart ahead of schedule. That is where The End of Patience begins, in a small, ordinary moment that feels strangely familiar. I keep noticing how little patience we seem to have left. Even the shortest lines feel unbearable; waiting begins to feel like a personal offense. Created for an independent publisher around the theme of “END,” this project became a way for me to reflect on urgency, discomfort, and the quiet anxiety of standing still. It takes the form of a multi-format editorial object, ephemeral, symbolic, and intentionally experimental.
